About ASTRA

Bridging Simulation and Reality

The ASTRA workshop explores the critical challenge of aligning AI systems trained in simulation with real-world deployment. We focus on three core pillars: World Models for accurate environment representation, Neuro-Symbolic Reasoning for interpretable decision-making, and Real-World Deployment strategies.

This workshop brings together researchers and practitioners to discuss state-of-the-art approaches, share experiences, and identify future research directions in sim-to-real alignment.

Research Tracks

World Models

Developing accurate models of environments for training and validation in simulation before real-world deployment.

Neuro-Symbolic AI

Combining neural networks with symbolic reasoning for interpretable and robust decision-making systems.

Transfer Learning

Techniques for transferring knowledge from simulation to real-world environments effectively.

Robustness & Safety

Ensuring AI systems remain safe and robust when transitioning from simulation to reality.

Robotics Applications

Real-world applications of sim-to-real alignment in robotic systems and autonomous agents.

Legal & Governance

Policy, ethics, and governance considerations for deploying AI systems in real-world scenarios.
